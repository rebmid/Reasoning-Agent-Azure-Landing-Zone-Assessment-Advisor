Insufficient Network Segmentation and Control

Design Area: Network Topology and Connectivity

Severity: High

If unresolved, all workloads in the visible subscription (f775e0e1-c710-47ae-9dee-f50a0e4a0c70) are exposed to lateral movement and potential external threats due to lack of network segmentation. This affects the entire management group scope, increasing the blast radius to all current and future resources in this subscription.

Course of Action: Implement a hub-and-spoke network topology using Azure Virtual WAN or Virtual Network Peering to centralize control and inspection. → Deploy and configure Azure Firewall or Network Security Groups (NSGs) at subnet and perimeter layers to enforce segmentation and restrict east-west and north-south traffic.

Unprotected Storage Resources Expose Sensitive Data

Design Area: Security

Severity: High

Unprotected storage accounts in the only visible subscription risk unauthorized access or data leakage, potentially impacting all workloads and data governed by the management group. This could result in regulatory violations and business data loss across the entire subscription.

Course of Action: Enable Azure Storage encryption (Azure Storage Service Encryption) and enforce HTTPS-only traffic on all storage accounts. → Apply Azure Policy to require private endpoints and block public network access for all storage accounts at the management group scope.

Excessive or Misaligned Privileged Access

Design Area: Identity and Access Management

Severity: High

Uncontrolled privileged access in the management group and subscription (f775e0e1-c710-47ae-9dee-f50a0e4a0c70) increases the risk of accidental or malicious changes, affecting all resources managed under this scope. This can lead to unauthorized deployments, data exposure, or service disruptions.

Course of Action: Review and remediate RBAC assignments at the management group and subscription level, removing unnecessary Owner or Contributor roles. → Implement Privileged Identity Management (PIM) for all privileged roles to enforce just-in-time access and access reviews.

Critical Security Baseline Controls Not Enforced

Design Area: Security

Severity: High

Without enforcement of baseline security controls, the entire subscription and management group are vulnerable to common attack vectors, increasing the likelihood of compromise or data loss across all governed workloads.

Course of Action: Assign Azure Security Benchmark policies at the management group level to enforce baseline security requirements. → Enable Microsoft Defender for Cloud to provide continuous security assessment and recommendations for all resources.

Inadequate Backup Coverage for Critical Resources

Design Area: Management

Severity: High

Lack of backup coverage exposes all resources in the visible subscription to data loss and extended outages in the event of accidental deletion, corruption, or ransomware. This affects the entire management group scope, jeopardizing business continuity.
