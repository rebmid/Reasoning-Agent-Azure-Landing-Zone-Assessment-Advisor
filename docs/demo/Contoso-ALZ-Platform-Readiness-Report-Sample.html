Platform Scale Implication

Contoso's current Azure platform maturity is critically low, with an overall maturity score below 20% and no design area exceeding 30% control pass rate. The assessment reveals systemic weaknesses in security, identity, data protection, and operational management, exposing the organization to significant business risks such as data loss, regulatory non-compliance, and service disruption. Most controls in key areas like data protection, resilience, and network security are failing or unimplemented, and there is a lack of foundational governance and cost controls. The most pressing risks are concentrated in the areas of data protection, network security, and privileged access management. These gaps create immediate threats to business continuity and reputation, as well as potential financial exposure from undetected breaches or uncontrolled costs. The investment thesis is clear: rapid remediation of these foundational gaps is essential to enable secure, scalable cloud adoption and to protect Contoso’s digital assets and brand. Without targeted investment in platform security, governance, and operational resilience, the organization remains vulnerable to both internal and external threats.

Top Platform Adoption Risks

Critical Data Exposure and Loss

ALZ Design Area: Security

Platform impact: Loss of sensitive data or intellectual property, regulatory fines, and erosion of customer trust.

Technical cause: Storage accounts and PaaS services lack required security controls (encryption, private endpoints); backup coverage is absent.

Network Breach and Service Disruption

ALZ Design Area: Network Topology and Connectivity

Platform impact: Potential for external attacks (e.g., DDoS), lateral movement, and uncontained outages impacting business operations.

Technical cause: No Azure Firewall, no DDoS protection, and lack of hub-spoke network segmentation.

Privilege Escalation and Unauthorized Access

ALZ Design Area: Identity and Access Management

Platform impact: Increased risk of accidental or malicious changes, audit failures, and breach of sensitive environments.

Technical cause: Excessive Owner role assignments, lack of Privileged Identity Management (PIM), and ineffective break-glass accounts.

Lack of Centralized Security Monitoring

ALZ Design Area: Management

Platform impact: Delayed detection and response to threats, leading to prolonged incidents and regulatory exposure.

Technical cause: Centralized security visibility is missing; SOC workspace topology is fragmented and lacks retention.

Uncontrolled Cloud Spend

ALZ Design Area: Governance

Platform impact: Budget overruns and inability to forecast or manage cloud costs, impacting profitability.

Technical cause: Absence of cost forecasting and governance controls.
